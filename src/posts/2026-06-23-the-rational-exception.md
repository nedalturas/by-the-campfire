We are far too occupied with measuring the faults of others that we forget to ask who we are beneath it all.



I found a certain comfort in solitude. Inside my head exists a world larger than any map could contain, though even there I am not entirely free. Still, I choose to keep it untouched, to wander its corridors a little longer.



The picture is not yet clear. The answers remain incomplete. But every quiet moment reveals another fragment of the person I am becoming.



I am at my most vulnerable when emotion takes command. Most mistakes begin there, not with ignorance, but with feeling.



Perhaps that is why the mind sits above the heart. One was built to experience the world; the other, to make sense of it.



So I let my thoughts take the lead. Not because they are flawless, but because they hesitate where emotions rush. And in a world where a single poor decision can alter everything, hesitation is often the wiser companion.Whenever I feel something concerning "us," my first reaction is alarm. It feels less like an emotion and more like stepping into unfamiliar territory without a map.



My mind immediately treats it as a threat—not because it is dangerous, but because it is unknown. I cannot yet observe it clearly, deduce its nature, or arrive at a satisfactory conclusion. And what cannot be understood cannot be trusted.



Perhaps that is why I cling so tightly to the code I have lived by:



"Love is a human error."



An anomaly in reason. A flaw in judgment. A force capable of turning the most rational mind into a willing accomplice to its own deception.



Yet the more I attempt to dismiss it as an error, the more I find myself investigating it. And every investigation begins the same way—with something that refuses to be explained.



For years, I mistook detachment for wisdom.



I became insensitive enough to hurt others and rational enough to justify it afterward. Their reactions were merely consequences. Their pain, a variable outside the scope of my concern.



That was the logic I lived by.



I am not a good man. Good men consider the damage they leave behind. I simply moved forward and called it necessity.



Yet there was one exception.



You.



The affection I carried for you was the only thing I never entrusted to that colder version of myself. Everything else was dissected, analyzed, and stripped of sentiment. But that feeling remained beyond the reach of reason.



A strange contradiction.



The monster I became rejected nearly everything human.



Except you.I love you enough to stay away — because the monster you need protection from is wearing my face.
