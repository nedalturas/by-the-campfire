Many are afraid to love, or even of love. The fear of being exposed, vulnerable to the highest degree. The act of jumping head first into the unknown. To encounter new people, places, and even events you never thought could take place. Afraid of the ever changing world, and the ever obscure intentions of people.

So we only watch from afar, the mountain peak of affection we know we could never reach. Wondering if it was ever worth the grueling climb. But it makes sense about how they are wary of it. To basically hand over a map of yourself, with your hidden bruises embedded on the map, shining like treasures to be exploited. Yet praying against all odds they won't. 

The anxiety in depending on a person who is not yourself. To build a home around them, only to find you were just a temporary stop. Then you find it suffocating to climb forward, as their blanket of comfort now shrouds your path. The pillar that supports you emotionally collapses. You sit in the rubble and debris, suffocated not by dust, but by your heart caving in.

The fragility of being understood in ways you never noticed. To be known inside and out, letting someone in on your world—a world full of hate, wrath, mistakes, and the flaws you abhor, locked deep within. For someone to know you're nothing but a beast incapable of being loved. Terrified to find that everything you feared about loving is true.

But oh, how her heart beats in sync with mine. How her touch traces a map to places I never knew existed within myself. How that smile illuminates paths my shadows never dared venture.

To be understood by her is not a vulnerability to be unearthed, but sanctuary. Each step together makes the grueling climb easier. Tethered by a quiet certainty that defies the obscure intentions of the world below. Hand in hand, we scale the rough edges of our skepticism, pushing past comfort until the air grows thin and clear. 

Against all odds, we've finally reached it, the view that we have longed for. We stood together at the very mountain peak of affection, looking out at a universe that suddenly makes sense. 

The Sun shines so bright at the peak, the world is enveloped with its golden radiance. The quiet at the top is peaceful, yet deafening. The view is magnificent, yet dizzying. Up here in the summit where everything I have longed for is, it clicked.

The higher you climb, the further there is to fall. To hold the universe in your hands means you finally have everything to lose. 

They say, "what is grief, if not love persevering?". That is a much more profound, bittersweet truth. It’s the realization that you cannot have light without its heat, and you cannot have a love this deep without the terrifying reality that it is fragile. Perhaps that is what I am most afraid of, and I have known it since.

Many are afraid of love, for the thought of losing everything you have is a burden no person can bear. I would rather be burned alive or torn to shreds, than to one day wake up without her embrace.

Now I see, the climb for the top was never to find a place for shelter, a guarantee that you won't be hurt by the fall, nor was it the illusion of healing the bruises and fatigue that were made before and during the climb. We braved the unknown, we stripped away the layers, and for a beautiful, fleeting moment, we both held the light. The loss does not erase the journey created, the trials faced with love and love alone. It proves that the view from the summit was worth every single step, as was the company.

As fleeting as we are, and as hurtful memories are, there's always a sliver of eternity that comes to loving. In this ever-changing, unforgiving world, being remembered is the closest thing that we can be to being eternal.
