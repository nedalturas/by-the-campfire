The office was loud.

Not with noise, but with performance.

Laughter timed too perfectly. Smiles stretched just enough to appear genuine. Compliments traded like currency between people terrified of becoming unnecessary.

I watched them quietly.

Humans often claim they fear loneliness, but that isn’t true. What they truly fear is irrelevance. Loneliness is merely the silence that exposes it.

A person can survive isolation.

But to realize nobody is waiting for your existence to matter?

That breaks people.

So they create versions of themselves for others to consume. A pleasant mask for friends. A dependable mask for family. A confident mask for strangers. Eventually they wear so many faces that even they forget which one was born first.

Perhaps that is why honesty feels so rare.

Not because humans enjoy lying...
but because they no longer recognize the truth within themselves.

I used to think people desired freedom.

I was wrong.

Freedom terrifies them.

Because freedom also means responsibility. No excuses. No script. No one to blame for failure except the reflection staring back from the mirror at midnight.

That is why most people willingly chain themselves to systems, groups, trends, and expectations. They call it belonging because the word sounds warmer than surrender.

But dependence always has a price.

The moment your happiness relies on something external, your life is no longer your own.

Love.
Recognition.
Status.
Validation.

Remove them, and most people collapse frighteningly fast.

Weakness is not the inability to fight.

Weakness is needing constant proof that you deserve to exist.
